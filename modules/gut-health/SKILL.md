## Symptom Timing & Patterns (added 6/28/26)
The food-diary/FODMAP frame assumes a *food* trigger. These observations point
partly elsewhere — toward motility, air-swallowing, and daily rhythm. Hold both:
SIBO/food may still matter, but a mechanical/rhythm layer is a separate,
independently fixable contributor.
- **Gas before eating, not just after.** Fermentation gas is post-meal; gas on an
  empty stomach is a motility / aerophagia / nervous-system signal. A pure food
  diary will keep coming up empty if it's the only lens.
- **Weekend-specific worsening,** clustering with reduced movement AND reduced
  appetite on those same days. That's not a food — it's a rhythm. Weekends remove
  the weekday structure (morning workout, set meals) that was quietly keeping
  things moving.
- **Daily structure as mechanical contributor:** morning workout → long stillness
  (desk, meetings) → eating concentrated at night. One movement spike, then ~10
  largely sedentary hours while everything downstream sits. Worst on heavy meeting
  days (Wed/Thu). Plausible driver of gas getting stuck, independent of SIBO.

## Splenic Flexure Episodes — Relief Protocol (added 6/28/26)
Acute episode 6/28: gas trapped at the left mid-section / under the left ribs,
consistent with **splenic flexure syndrome** (gas hung up at the bend in the colon
where transverse meets descending). What actually gave relief in the moment —
keep this, it's hard-won and counter to the generic advice:
- **Lie on the RIGHT side** — NOT the left. General "lie on your left for gas"
  advice is for stomach/small-intestine gas; for splenic-flexure gas, right-side
  lying changes the geometry so gas can climb out of the bend. Tiffany forced
  left-side for years on generic advice while her body was signaling the opposite.
  Trust the body here (data informs, doesn't override intuition).
- **Gentle tapping / pressure** on the left side.
- **Legs-up "V" position with pulsing leg movement** — she also used to do this
  exact leg-pulse/rotational movement for toning. Rotational movement appears to
  move gas through better than static stretching.

## Movement as Dual-Purpose (added 6/28/26)

a gap the current treadmill + strength split doesn't fully cover. Recommendations

  even minimally. These are the least flexible days and where the "if I get up I'll
  lose momentum" pull is strongest.

  thread" momentum feeling may be worth noticing in other contexts too, not just
